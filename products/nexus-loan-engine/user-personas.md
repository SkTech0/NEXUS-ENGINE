# Nexus Loan Engine — User Personas

---

## 1. The applicant

- **Goal**: Get a loan decision quickly and understand the outcome.  
- **Needs**: Clear approve/decline/refer, reason in plain language, optional next steps.  
- **Behavior**: Applies via lender’s channel; may check status or appeal if allowed.  
- **Success**: Receives a fair, understandable decision without unnecessary delay.  

## 2. The lending operations manager

- **Goal**: Run daily decision volume with consistency and control.  
- **Needs**: Reliable decisions, configurable policy, referral handling, basic monitoring.  
- **Behavior**: Configures product, monitors queues, handles referrals and overrides.  
- **Success**: Volume is handled with fewer errors and full auditability.  

## 3. The risk / credit policy owner

- **Goal**: Set and enforce credit and risk policy through the product.  
- **Needs**: Policy levers (limits, rules, referral thresholds), explainability, compliance support.  
- **Behavior**: Defines policy in product terms; reviews outcomes and overrides.  
- **Success**: Policy is applied consistently; regulators and auditors can verify.  

## 4. The integration / tech owner (lender)

- **Goal**: Embed Loan Engine into origination or app with minimal friction.  
- **Needs**: Clear integration contract, docs, and support; no surprise behavior.  
- **Behavior**: Integrates API, tests flows, monitors in production.  
- **Success**: Loan Engine is live in their flow with stable behavior.  

## 5. The regulator / auditor

- **Goal**: Verify that decisions are fair, consistent, and explainable.  
- **Needs**: Audit trail, explanation per decision, policy visibility (as allowed).  
- **Behavior**: Reviews reports, samples, or export; may ask for specific decision trace.  
- **Success**: Can attest that the product supports explainability and compliance.  

---

*Personas define who the product serves. They inform flows, features, and prioritization—not implementation details.*
