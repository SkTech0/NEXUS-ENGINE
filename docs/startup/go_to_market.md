# Nexus Engine — Go-to-Market

GTM strategy, segments, channels, and milestones.

---

## 1. Target Segments

### 1.1 Primary

| Segment | Profile | Motion | Priority |
|---------|---------|--------|----------|
| **Dev / platform teams** | In-house platform, APIs, data/AI | PLG, API-first, docs | P1 |
| **Vertical SaaS / ISVs** | Building multi-tenant product | PLG + sales; multi-tenant, billing | P1 |
| **Scale-ups** | Post-PMF; need compliance and scale | Sales-assisted; enterprise features | P2 |

### 1.2 Secondary

| Segment | Profile | Motion |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **Enterprises** | Regulated, SLA, compliance | Direct sales; compliance_engine, sla_manager |
| **Ecosystem / builders** | Plugins, integrations | Marketplace, docs, partner program |

---

## 2. Channels

### 2.1 Product-led

- **API and docs**: Onboarding, quickstart, and reference; usage and limits (usage_tracker, license_manager).
- **Product UI**: Dashboard, engine monitor, AI console — activation and stickiness.
- **Self-serve signup**: Free or trial tier; upgrade path to paid (subscription_service, pricing_engine).
- **Metrics**: Signups, activation, time-to-value, conversion to paid.

### 2.2 Sales and partnerships

- **Inbound**: Leads from product, content, and events; qualification and demo.
- **Outbound**: Target accounts (vertical, size); outreach and pilots.
- **Partners**: Integrators, resellers, marketplace; co-sell and referral.
- **Metrics**: Pipeline, conversion, ACV, cycle time.

### 2.3 Marketing and community

- **Content**: Use cases, benchmarks, and best practices; SEO and nurture.
- **Community**: Forums, Slack/Discord, or open-source; feedback and advocacy.
- **Events**: Webinars, conferences, and workshops; demand and brand.

---

## 3. Phased GTM

### Phase 1: Launch and early adopters

- **Goal**: First paying tenants; validate positioning and packaging.
- **Actions**: Private beta; early design partners; pricing and packaging tests.
- **Success**: N paying tenants; documented use cases; NPS or qualitative feedback.

### Phase 2: Scale PLG

- **Goal**: Repeatable self-serve acquisition and conversion.
- **Actions**: Public launch; improved onboarding and docs; usage-based and tiered pricing.
- **Success**: MRR growth rate; activation and conversion rates; CAC and LTV trends.

### Phase 3: Sales and enterprise

- **Goal**: Higher-ACV and enterprise deals; compliance and SLA attach.
- **Actions**: Enterprise tier and packages; compliance_engine, sla_manager, policy_engine positioning; sales playbook and partners.
- **Success**: Enterprise ARR; NRR; average contract size and duration.

### Phase 4: Platform and ecosystem

- **Goal**: Marketplace and ecosystem revenue; network effects.
- **Actions**: Marketplace_engine and partner program; plugin and integration catalog; revenue share and co-marketing.
- **Success**: Marketplace GMV; number of partners and listings; ecosystem revenue.

---

## 4. Positioning and Messaging

- **Headline**: Unified platform for distributed intelligence, trust, and monetization.
- **Problem**: Fragmented tools, integration debt, delayed revenue and compliance.
- **Solution**: One stack — engines, API, UI, SaaS, monetization, enterprise.
- **Proof**: Traction, benchmarks, case studies, and testimonials (update as available).

---

## 5. Competitive and Differentiation

- **vs point solutions**: Single platform; less glue; consistent trust and billing.
- **vs build-your-own**: Faster ship; built-in multi-tenant, billing, compliance.
- **vs large cloud suites**: Focused on “engine” layer; modular; developer-first and API-first.
